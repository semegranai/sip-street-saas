# Sip Steet Master Roadmap

**Company Vision**: To build a **nationwide, vertically integrated real estate development company** that owns the entire value chain — from land acquisition and SIP manufacturing to construction, sales, and asset management.  
**Core Differentiation**: SIP (Structural Insulated Panels) technology as the competitive advantage — delivering faster construction (40-60% time savings), lower costs (20-35% savings), superior energy efficiency, and higher quality control.

**Last Updated**: April 7, 2026  
**Current Stage**: Phase 1 – Internal Financial Modeling Tool (Sip Steet)

---

## Executive Summary

Sip Steet Group will operate as a **vertically integrated developer-builder-manufacturer**.  
By owning the SIP manufacturing company, we gain:
- Preferred pricing and priority production for our own projects
- Ability to sell SIP panels to other builders (additional revenue stream)
- Full control over quality, lead times, and innovation

We will develop across **all major asset classes**:
- Residential (subdivisions, townhomes, single-family, multifamily)
- Commercial (office, retail, industrial/warehouse)
- Hospitality (hotels, boutique inns, short-term rental communities)
- Mission-critical (data centers, cold storage, healthcare facilities)

## Key Risks & Mitigation Strategies (Lessons from Failed AI/Vibe-Coded SaaS)

1. **No Product-Market Fit** → Mandatory Phase 0 validation with real developers
2. **Technical Debt** → Move from single HTML file to proper Next.js + Supabase architecture in Phase 2
3. **Inconsistent Calculations** → All modules must share the same calculation engine
4. **Over-complicated UI** → Prioritize simplicity and clarity (current 2-tab approach is good)
5. **Ignoring Local Cost Realism** → Continue refining sub-component defaults based on real KC/Raytown data
6. **Security & Data Privacy** → Proper user authentication and project isolation when adding cloud features
